# Product

## Register

product

## Users

Friend groups in their 20s–30s planning trips together. They open TravelSync in a social context — excited, already talking about the trip — and want a tool that matches that energy without slowing them down. One person usually drives the session; the rest look on or jump in. The primary job: turn scattered ideas into a real itinerary, fast.

## Product Purpose

TravelSync is the AI-assisted trip planning arm of the SubSync ecosystem. Users create a trip, brainstorm ideas on a shared canvas, generate an AI-structured itinerary, then refine and share it. It exists to compress the gap between "we should go somewhere" and "here's the plan" — removing coordination overhead so the group can stay excited rather than getting bogged down in logistics.

## Brand Personality

**Three words:** Capable. Considered. Ready.

The tone is confident without being cold. The app feels like it was built by someone who travels and actually uses the thing — opinionated UI choices, clear hierarchy, nothing decorative that doesn't earn its place. References: Linear's considered precision, Notion's quiet capability. The planning experience should feel as satisfying as the trip itself.

## Anti-references

- **Generic SaaS/startup aesthetic** — no navy-blue hero zones, gradient CTAs, hero illustrations, or metric-grid layouts. This is a consumer tool for excited travelers, not a B2B product.
- **"Powered by AI" as a design element** — the AI is a feature, not the identity. No purple gradients, no glassmorphism, no chip badges announcing intelligence.
- **OTA booking density** — not Expedia. No stock photography collages, no dense card grids with star ratings.

## Design Principles

1. **The tool disappears** — interface friction should never be noticeable; only the trip should feel real.
2. **Collaborative by default** — every decision should work for a group looking at a screen together, not just a solo user.
3. **Confidence through hierarchy** — clear visual priority tells the user exactly what to do next; no guessing, no equal-weight layouts.
4. **Earned moments** — delight only where it matters (confirmation, empty states, success). Not as wallpaper.
5. **Sharp over safe** — opinionated choices over crowd-pleasing neutrality. A clear design voice beats attempting to please everyone.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

WCAG AA: 4.5:1 contrast for body text, 3:1 for large/bold text. Keyboard navigable. Meaningful ARIA labels on interactive elements. All animations must have `@media (prefers-reduced-motion: reduce)` alternatives.
